Spectrometers:

Helium Nanodroplet Isolation Spectroscopy

HENDI is a form of matrix isolation where the host, a beam of helium droplets of 103 to 104 atoms, is formed by adiabatic expansion of helium from a cold nozzle into a vacuum.

Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy

An FTIR spectrometer simultaneously collects high-resolution spectral data over a wide spectral range.

Balle-Flygare type Cavity Fourier Transform Microwave Spectrometer

Microwave spectroscopy is the spectroscopy method that employs microwaves, i.e. electromagnetic radiation at GHz frequencies, for the study of matter.

Standard Photoacoustic Spectrometer

Photoacoustic spectroscopy is the measurement of the effect of absorbed electromagnetic energy (particularly of light) on matter by means of acoustic detection.

Research

Introduction

We use a variety of spectroscopic techniques in order to satisfy our curiosity of the physical world. These techniques are high in resolution, and span the lowish end of the electromagnetic spectrum (microwave to infrared). We use them to investigate small molecules and complexes in an effort to improve our understanding of atmospheric chemistry, astrochemistry, combustion chemistry, and superfluidity.
